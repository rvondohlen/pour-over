How to make great pour over coffee

  • Gather Items

    To make 1 cup of coffee you'll need:

    • Water - approximately 2 cups or 500 grams
    • Kettle with Thermometer - for heating the water
    • Kitchen Scale - for measuring beans and water
    • Coffee Beans - 3 tbsp. or 22-30 grams, whole beans
    • Pour-over Funnel - I use a Hario V60
    • Paper Filter - I use matching Hario V60 02 Cone Filters
    • Grinder - I use a Bodum Electric Blade
    • Empty Vessel - a container large enough for your coffee

  • Boil Water

    First, set the kettle, filled with about 2 cups of water, to boil over medium-high heat.

  • Grind Coffee

    Measure 22-30 grams of beans and grind into fine, sand-like granules.

  • Wet Filter

    When the water reaches 205°, place a new filter in the funnel and pour enough water through to wet most of the filter. This removes any unwanted papery tastes that may come from a dry filter.

    Pour out the water.

  • Add Grounds

    Add grounds to wet filter. Tap to level and add the funnel to the empty cup.

    Place on the scale and tare.

  • Bloom Pour

    Pouring in a circular motion from the outside edge, moving in, add 60 grams of water over 15 seconds.

    Wait 30 seconds for funnel to drain.

    0:45 Start

  • Second Pour

    From the center out and back again, pour 90 grams of water over 20 seconds.

    Wait 25 seconds.

    0:45 Start

    I consider my grind perfect if the water just reaches the top of the grounds as this timer expires.

  • Third Pour

    Repeating the same motion, pour 100 grams of water over 20 seconds.

    0:20 Start

    Wait until the water reaches the top of the grounds.

  • Fourth Pour

    Pour a second 100 grams of water over 20 seconds.

    0:20 Start

    Wait until all of the fresh coffee drains, serve and enjoy.
